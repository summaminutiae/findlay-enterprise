Findlay to host band festival

Findlay High School will host a music
festival on Tuesday, Nov. 8. Six area high
schools will pool their concert bands for a
band festival to be held in the Findlay
High School gym.

One-hundred-thirty band students
from Cowden-Herrick Hi Schoo
Nokomis High School,
Hammond High School, Effingham St.
Anthony High School, Ramsey High
School and Findlay High School will
spend the entire day Tuesday rehearsing
for an evening concert.

The bands will be rehearsing under the
direction of Harold L. Hillyer for an
evening concert scheduled for 7:30 p.m.
The concert is open to the public.

Hillyer is on the staff of Eastern
Illinois University at Charleston. In the

12 years he has been associated with

Eastern, he has directed the E.L.U. Wind
Ensemble, the Marching Blue of E.1.U.,
he is a member of the College Band
Directors National Association, the
Music Educators National Conference,
Phi Beta Mu, the American School Band
Association, Association of
Bands of America, Kappa
Kappa Psi (national honorary band
fraternity), Tau Beta Sigma (national
honorary band sorority), Illinois Music
Educators Association, American Band
Masters Association and he is listed in
\[OpenCurlyDoubleQuote]Who's Who in American Education,\[CloseCurlyDoubleQuote]
Hillyer has served as Clinician, ad-
judicator, lecturer and guest conductor.
He is currently teaching applied trum-
pet, brass classes and is a student
teaching supervisor for the Eastern
inois University School of Music.
